## Course Overview

AER 315 introduces the fundamental principles governing combustion processes and their applications in aerospace and energy systems. The course covers thermodynamics, chemical kinetics, flame propagation, combustion devices, and environmental impacts associated with fuel utilization.

Students will:

- Analyze thermodynamic processes in reacting systems
- Calculate combustion temperatures and equilibrium product compositions
- Apply chemical kinetics principles to combustion reactions
- Understand laminar and turbulent flame behavior
- Study combustion phenomena in engines, gas turbines, furnaces, and rockets
- Evaluate pollutant formation processes and environmental impacts
- Compare conventional and alternative fuels for transportation applications

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of the course, students will be able to:

- Differentiate between various combustion modes
- Apply the first and second laws of thermodynamics to reacting systems
- Estimate combustion temperatures and gas compositions
- Perform basic chemical kinetics analyses relevant to combustion
- Calculate properties of laminar flames and relate them to turbulent combustion
- Explain operating principles of aerospace and transportation propulsion systems
- Assess the advantages and limitations of conventional and bio-based fuels
- Describe pollutant formation mechanisms and environmental consequences
